The "Bible"? Hebrew Bible Apocrypha New Testament

The Bible, rather than being a book in the traditional sense we think of, is a collection of writings by different authors, from different time periods, with different views and agendas.

These books are usually organized into three major sections:

  • the Hebrew Bible (generally known to Christians as the Old Testament),
  • the Apocrypha (which tends to be sort of pushed aside), and
  • the New Testament (the part written by early Christians).

The Hebrew Bible, known to Christians as the Old Testament, is a collection of ancient Hebrew writings, most of them composed in the 8th through 2nd centuries BCE, mostly in ancient Hebrew, with some of the later parts composed in Aramaic.

Topics, points of view, even genres vary, from the mythological and legendary tales such as those found in Genesis and Exodus, to more historical narratives of Samuel and Kings, to prophetical verse or prose such as in Isaiah or Ezekiel, to the praiseful poems of Psalms, to short stories such as Esther or Ruth, and plenty others.
